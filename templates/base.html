About

The Environmental Management System is a system and database which integrates procedures and processes for training of personnel, monitoring, summarizing and reporting of the performance of the organization on the Environment to internal and external stakeholders of the firm.

The goals of EMS are two fold. The first is to increase the compliance of the organization with the existing environment laws. This portion of the system aims to keep with the existing minimal legal standards. Not being compliant to any of them may result in fines and government intervention in the business and even inability to operate in the industry. That’s why it is very important for the industry to closely observe these laws and is like one of things to be bound to by the organization. The second portion of the system involves the reduction of the waste produced by the organization. In this portion we go beyond compliance and aim towards reduction of waste and hence damage to the ecosystem. EMS helps to develop, implement, manage, coordinate and monitor, environment policies. In common practice, waste reduction begins at design phase with reduction of pollution and waste reduction and in the end of life cycle, the waste is reduced by recycling.
